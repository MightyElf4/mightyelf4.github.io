Background

Born and raised in Fort Worth, Texas, I spent most of my first 23 years of life around the DFW area. In May of 2025, I graduated from the University of Texas at Arlington with a Bachelor of Arts in English (and a minor in technical communication). In March of 2026, I left DFW to serve as an English education volunteer with Peace Corps Costa Rica (PCCR).

I am the middle of three children, the child of two hard working parents, and a self-designated "glue guy". Growing up, I was blessed to not only be given opportunity, but to be given role-models. Looking to, leaning on, and learning from the people in my life inspired me to want to dedicate my life to helping others.

Service

In Costa Rica, my job is not only to teach English, but to be a human resource and understand the needs of my community in order to help create sustainable change. Service is simultaneously a challenge and an opportunity, and I am committed to making the most of it.

The Three Goals of the Peace Corps

  1. To help the countries interested in meeting their need for trained people.
  2. To help promote a better understanding of Americans on the part of the peoples served.
  3. To help promote a better understanding of other peoples on the part of Americans.

For me, service has already had such a positive impact on my life. Costa Rica is a beautiful country, and I have made many meaningful connections here. For me, service is not only about helping others, but additionally, helping me increase and reinforce my ability to do so. After service ends, I plan on continuing my education and continuing to be a resource for my community.
